Hello, I'm Anne Lee Steele.

I'm a MA candidate at the Graduate Institute of International and Development Studies, Geneva, and a Reproducible Research Fellow at the Open Knowledge Foundation. In my free time, I curate supply-chains.us and open-source-social-science.

For me, research and multimedia are inextricably linked. As an anthropologist, I'm interested in the people & processes behind our digital lives (think: open source software, maps, and supply chains). Through writing, design, and digital art, I'm always trying to make these ideas accessible and engaging.

Before moving to Switzerland, I researched conservation in Bhutan, co-led student expeditions in Nepal, and coded data visualizations for a Washington DC think tank. As an undergraduate at Columbia University, I studied global cities and natural resource conflict. I also designed media for different organizations in New York City.

When I'm not on a computer, I'm usually outside.
