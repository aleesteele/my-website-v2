I'm Anne Lee Steele, currently a MA candidate at the Graduate Institute of International and Development Studies, Geneva.

Before moving to Switzerland, I researched conservation in Bhutan, lead student expeditions in Nepal, and coded data visualizations for a Washington DC think tank. As an undergraduate at Columbia University, I studied global cities and natural resource conflict. I'm interested in how technology and the environment affect people and their politics, and I've written about how important those in the middle are for mitigating such complicated problems.

These experiences fueled my passion for interdisciplinary research, and taught me the importance of using modern tools for my work. But when I'm not thinking about digital ethnography or human-centered design, I'm either in the mountains, writing, or shooting video.
